Overview

I designed and fully constrained every component of this can opener, then validated the design through simulation and produced a complete manufacturing drawing package.

  • Produced detailed 2D engineering drawings, a fully mated assembly, exploded views, and a complete drawing package for all components.
  • Conducted static Finite Element Analysis (FEA) on components under operational loading, validating the design against stainless steel's yield strength.
  • Performed motion analysis on the gear train, quantifying mechanical advantage and contact force distribution across a full rotation cycle.
  • Designed the gear tooth profile, frame geometry, and ergonomic handle form.

Design Process

1

Concept & component breakdown

Identified each part — handle body, second arm, drive gear, feed gear, cutting wheel, screws, knob, and frame — and defined how they mate.

2

Part modeling in SolidWorks

Modeled each component individually, including the involute gear tooth profile and the ergonomic handle.

3

Assembly & mating

Built a fully constrained assembly with zero degrees of freedom error, then generated exploded views.

4

FEA & motion analysis

Ran static FEA under operating loads and a motion study across a full rotation to characterize mechanical advantage and gear contact forces.

5

Drawing package

Delivered dimensioned 2D drawings and a complete documentation set ready for manufacture.
