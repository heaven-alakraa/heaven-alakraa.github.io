Cantilever Beam Deflection Scale

MATLAB Fabrication BDW

A cantilever beam–based mechanical scale that translates Euler–Bernoulli beam theory and Hooke's Law into a working physical prototype.

Overview

I designed and fabricated a mechanical scale, applying analytical deflection expressions to predict mass from beam displacement, then validated the design experimentally.

  • Fabricated using a band saw, drill press, and other tools in the Brown Design Workshop.
  • Optimized beam dimensions and load placement in MATLAB — computing spring constant, bending stress, and safety factor.
  • Experimentally validated through incremental calibration, predicting unknown masses with less than 10% error.

Design Process

1

Analytical model

Derived tip deflection from Euler–Bernoulli beam theory and related it to applied mass via the beam's spring constant.

2

MATLAB optimization

Swept beam length, thickness, and material to balance sensitivity against bending stress and a target safety factor.

3

Fabrication

Cut and drilled the beam and mount in the Brown Design Workshop, then assembled the deflection indicator.

4

Calibration & validation

Calibrated with known masses and tested against unknowns, achieving under 10% prediction error.
