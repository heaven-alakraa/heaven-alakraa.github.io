RNA Purification Tube Redesign

SolidWorks Design for Filtration 3D Printing Biotech

A product-design project at Lilac Biosciences to rethink how RNA is purified — replacing the centrifuge step with filtration built directly into the tube. Currently at the concept and CAD stage.

● Ongoing — current work

The problem

Conventional RNA purification relies on spin columns: the sample is loaded into a tube and a centrifuge forces it through a membrane to separate RNA from the rest of the lysate. It works, but it ties every purification to a centrifuge — bulky lab equipment, batch-limited throughput, and an extra hands-on step for every sample. The goal of this project is to remove that dependency.

The approach

Instead of relying on centrifugal force, the redesign integrates the filtration directly into the tube's geometry, so separation can happen without spinning. The engineering challenge is shaping the internal flow path and membrane seat so the sample passes through the filter cleanly and the RNA is retained — all within a form factor that's still easy to handle and cheap enough to be disposable.

  • Redesigning the tube geometry in SolidWorks around an integrated filtration path rather than a centrifuge-driven one.
  • Evaluating filtration-membrane candidates — pore size and material compatibility for reliable RNA retention.
  • Planning 3D-printed prototypes to test the geometry and iterate quickly.

Where it stands

The project is at the concept stage: I've modeled the rough external geometry of the tube in SolidWorks and am now working inward — defining the internal filtration path, the membrane seat, and the flow features. Next steps are finalizing the internal detail, selecting a membrane, and printing the first prototypes for flow testing.
